ATS Resume Tips for Freshers and College Students in India

AP
ATSPro Editorial Team
|1,350 words

Getting your first job in India has never been more competitive — and ATS software is a massive part of the challenge that most college students and fresh graduates simply don't know exists. Whether you're applying to TCS, Infosys, Wipro, or a funded startup, there's a very high chance your resume goes through an automated filter before any human sees it. The good news: as a fresher, your resume can absolutely score well on ATS — if you know what to include and how to structure it.

How ATS Works in the Indian Job Market

The Indian job market uses ATS across both mass recruiters and premium employers. Here's how different types of employers use it:

  • Service companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL, Cognizant): These companies process tens of thousands of applications through proprietary ATS portals. They filter heavily on CGPA cutoffs (typically 60–65% or 6.0–6.5 CGPA), specific programming language mentions, and degree relevance.
  • Product companies (Google India, Microsoft India, Flipkart, Swiggy, Zepto): Use global ATS platforms like Greenhouse or Workday. They look for tech stack keywords, project quality indicators, and competitive programming involvement.
  • Startups (YC-backed, Sequoia portfolio companies): Often use Lever or similar. More flexibility in format, but keywords and clear project outcomes still matter.
  • Naukri.com and LinkedIn India: Have their own ranking systems that function like ATS. Your profile/resume "relevance score" determines if you appear in recruiter searches. Keyword optimization for these platforms follows the same principles as traditional ATS.

Education Section — Your Strongest Card as a Fresher

For freshers, Education is often the most important section — place it before Work Experience if your work history is limited. Include the following for each educational qualification:

  • Degree name (e.g., B.Tech, B.E., B.Sc) and specialization (e.g., Computer Science and Engineering)
  • University/Institution name in full (many ATS systems have databases of recognized institutions)
  • Graduation year
  • CGPA or Percentage (if above the industry threshold — typically 7.0+ CGPA or 65%+)
  • Relevant coursework if applicable (Data Structures, Computer Networks, DBMS, Operating Systems for CS)

Also include your 12th and 10th marks if above 75%, especially for service company applications which often use these as hard filters.

Skills Section for Indian Freshers

Your skills section is your primary keyword vehicle when you lack work experience. Be specific and use full, official names of technologies:

CC++JavaPythonJavaScriptHTML/CSSReactMySQLMongoDBGitGitHubData StructuresAlgorithmsOOPREST APIsLinux

Only list skills you actually have — you may be asked to demonstrate any of them in an interview. A common mistake is listing "Machine Learning" or "TensorFlow" after doing a single Coursera tutorial. If you're genuinely working on learning it, add it with a qualifier like "Machine Learning (learning)".

Substituting Experience with Projects

Projects are the fresher's equivalent of work experience. ATS systems can't tell the difference between "Built at a startup" and "Built as a college project" — both can use the same format and rank similarly on keyword matching. For each project:

  • Give it a clear, descriptive title (not "My Project 1")
  • Write one sentence explaining what it does and who it's for
  • List the tech stack as keywords (React | Node.js | MongoDB)
  • Add at least one quantified detail: "Handles 500+ product entries," "Reduces processing time by 40%," "Used by 200+ users"
  • Include the GitHub link if the code is publicly available and presentable

Strong Projects for Freshers (Indian Market Focus):

  • Full-stack CRUD applications (any business use case)
  • Competitive programming solutions repository on GitHub (shows DSA skills)
  • API integrations (using Razorpay, UPI APIs for fintech roles)
  • Data analysis projects using Python and Pandas on Indian datasets
  • Chatbots or NLP projects using Python/NLTK for the Indian language space

Presenting Internship Experience

If you've done a college internship — even a summer internship of 1–2 months — include it in your work experience section. Don't diminish its importance by calling it "Trainee" or "Intern" if you can describe the actual work. Use the same bullet point formula: action verb + technology + quantified result.

If you did a virtual internship (through platforms like Internshala, LetsUpgrade, or NASSCOM Futureskills), include it with the platform name and the skills you worked with. These are legitimate credentials that ATS will recognize.

Certifications that Boost Fresher ATS Scores

Certifications signal initiative and structured learning. These are most valuable for freshers in the Indian market:

  • Google Cloud Skill Boost / ACE: Very well-recognized by Indian MNCs and Google India
  • AWS Cloud Practitioner: Great for cloud-adjacent roles
  • Infosys Springboard Certifications: Directly recognized by Infosys ATS
  • NPTEL Certifications: Recognized by IIT-affiliated companies and PSUs
  • Coursera / edX (IBM, Google, DeepLearning.AI): High credibility for product companies
  • HackerRank / LeetCode certifications: Useful for showing DSA proficiency

Optimal Resume Format for Indian Freshers

For freshers in India, a one-page resume is standard and expected. Companies like TCS and Infosys explicitly prefer concise one-page resumes for entry-level roles. Here's the optimal section order:

  1. Contact Information (Name, Phone, Email, LinkedIn, GitHub)
  2. Professional Objective / Summary (2–3 sentences tailored to the role)
  3. Education (CGPA displayed prominently)
  4. Technical Skills (organized by category)
  5. Projects (2–3 strong projects with tech stack and outcomes)
  6. Internships / Work Experience (if any)
  7. Certifications
  8. Achievements / Extra-Curricular (hackathon wins, leadership roles, cultural fest)
⚠️

Avoid listing "team player," "passionate," "hardworking," and "quick learner" as standalone bullet points in your resume. These phrases are invisible to ATS and meaningless to recruiters. Prove these qualities through specific project and achievement bullet points instead.

Final Tips for Fresher Job Seekers in India

The Indian job market in 2026 is fiercely competitive at the entry level — but it is also full of opportunity for candidates who understand how hiring actually works. ATS filtering is the first hurdle, but it's also the most systematic and therefore the most learnable. Optimize your education section, build 2–3 strong technical projects, earn 2–3 relevant certifications, and use ATSPro to check your resume score before every major application batch. Small improvements to keyword coverage and formatting can dramatically change your shortlisting rate.
