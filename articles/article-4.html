Best Resume Format for ATS in 2026 — Complete Guide

AP
ATSPro Editorial Team
|1,400 words

The way your resume looks matters far less than how it reads — to a machine. Before a recruiter ever sees your beautifully designed document, an ATS has already stripped out most of the formatting and judged you purely on text. Understanding the formatting rules that ATS systems enforce is the foundation of every successful job application in 2026.

The Three Resume Formats — Which Works Best for ATS?

Chronological (Reverse Chronological) — Best for ATS

The reverse-chronological format lists your work experience from most recent to oldest. This is universally the best format for ATS because it aligns perfectly with how ATS systems are designed to parse experience data. ATS parsers expect to find your most recent job first, extract the company, title, dates, and bullet points, then move to the previous job and repeat.

Reverse-chronological resumes score higher in most ATS systems because the parsing algorithm can correctly populate all experience fields. They also make it easy for the recruiter to quickly see your career trajectory once the resume reaches a human.

Functional (Skills-Based) — Poorly Suited for ATS

The functional format groups your experience by skill category rather than chronologically. While it was briefly popular to help hide employment gaps, this format is a disaster for ATS. Parsers expect dates and company names with each job entry. When they encounter skill-based sections without chronological markers, they struggle to categorize the data — often leaving your experience section incomplete or mis-categorized in the ATS database.

Avoid functional resumes for any role where ATS is likely to be involved. That means nearly every online application in 2026.

Combination (Hybrid) — Acceptable with Caution

A hybrid format leads with a skills or summary section and follows with chronological experience. This can work for ATS if the experience section remains purely chronological and the skills section uses clear, standard formatting. Keep it simple — if the hybrid layout becomes complex, default to pure chronological.

✅

ATS Verdict: Use reverse-chronological for 95% of job applications. It's the standard format that every ATS system is built to parse correctly.

Best Fonts for ATS Resumes

ATS systems parse text, not fonts — but choosing the wrong font can still cause issues. Some decorative fonts embed text as paths rather than characters in PDFs, making them unreadable by text parsers. Stick to universally supported, highly legible fonts:

Margins and Spacing

Margins should be between 0.5 and 1 inch on all sides. Going below 0.5 inches makes the document look cramped and can cause printing or PDF-conversion issues that affect parsing. Going above 1 inch wastes valuable space you need for content.

Line spacing: 1.0 to 1.15 within paragraphs, with a small space before each new section. Consistent spacing helps ATS parsers identify section boundaries. Avoid excessive blank lines, which can confuse section detection algorithms.

File Type — PDF vs DOCX

The short answer: DOCX is safer for ATS; PDF is better for humans.

DOCX files are designed to be parsed by software and give ATS systems the clearest, most structured text extraction. PDF is more "fixed" and some older ATS platforms struggle with PDF formatting, especially if tables or multi-column layouts are used. However, modern ATS platforms (Workday, Greenhouse, Lever) handle PDF well when the file is text-based (not image/scanned).

Rule of thumb: If the application instructions don't specify, submit DOCX for ATS-heavy applications (large company portals). Use PDF when sending directly to a person or when the format must be preserved precisely.

The Ideal Section Order for ATS

Section order matters because ATS parsers weight differently positioned information and recruiters have limited review time. Here's the recommended order in 2026:

  1. Contact Information — Always first, always in the body (not a header)
  2. Professional Summary — 2–4 sentence executive summary with target keywords
  3. Skills — Keyword-dense section, highly scannable
  4. Work Experience — Reverse chronological, most recent first
  5. Education — Degree, institution, year. GPA only if above 3.5 and within 3 years
  6. Certifications — Full certification names with issuing body
  7. Projects — Optional, valuable for early-career and tech roles
  8. Volunteer Work / Publications — Optional, if relevant to role
⚠️

Exception for new graduates: Put Education before Work Experience if your degree is your strongest qualification. For everyone with 2+ years of relevant experience, work experience should come before education.

Formatting Elements to Avoid Completely

  • ❌ Multi-column layouts
  • ❌ Tables for organizing experience or skills
  • ❌ Text boxes
  • ❌ Headers and footers (in the Word sense)
  • ❌ Images, graphics, icons, logos
  • ❌ Colored backgrounds or shaded sections
  • ❌ Creative/decorative bullets (use standard • only)
  • ❌ Hyperlinks that are long URLs (use short display text)
  • ❌ Skill rating bars or visual scales

Ideal Resume Length by Experience Level

  • 0–2 years experience: 1 page maximum
  • 3–7 years experience: 1–2 pages
  • 8–15 years experience: 2 pages
  • 15+ years / Executive roles: 2–3 pages maximum
  • Academic CVs: No page limits apply

Your ATS resume template should follow this pattern at the top:

[Degree], [Major] — [University Name] | [Graduation Year]

Conclusion

The ideal ATS resume format in 2026 is not the most creative or visually impressive — it's the one that ATS systems can parse most accurately and completely. Prioritize reverse-chronological structure, standard section headers, ATS-safe fonts, simple formatting, and the right file type. Once you've built your ATS-optimized base resume, you can create a beautiful visual version for networking and direct applications.
