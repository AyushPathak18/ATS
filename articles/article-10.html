Complete ATS Resume Checklist for 2026

✅ File Format Checklist

  • ✅
    Submitted in the correct format — If the application specifies DOCX, use DOCX. If no preference stated, DOCX is safest. PDF is also fine if text-based.
  • ✅
    Not an image-based PDF — Test by selecting and copying text from the PDF. If you can't select text, it's image-based and will fail ATS parsing.
  • ✅
    Not in an unsupported format — No .pages, .odt, .rtf, .jpg, .png, Google Docs share links, or ZIP files.
  • ✅
    File is named professionally — Format: FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf. No spaces, no version numbers visible to employer.
  • ✅
    File size is under 10MB — Though most ATS accept files up to 5–10MB, smaller is faster and safer.

✅ Contact Information Checklist

  • ✅
    Full legal name at the top of the document, not in a header/footer.
  • ✅
    Professional email address — firstname.lastname@gmail.com format. No nicknames, birth years in email, or creative email addresses.
  • ✅
    Phone number with country code — Include +91 or +1 if applying internationally. Must be a number you actually answer.
  • ✅
    City and state/country — Full address not required, but location is mandatory for most ATS geo-filtering.
  • ✅
    LinkedIn profile URL — Use your custom vanity URL (linkedin.com/in/yourname). Make sure the profile is up to date and complete.
  • ✅
    GitHub / Portfolio URL (for technical roles) — Clean, professional URL with relevant projects visible.
  • ✅
    All contact info is in the document body, not in Word header/footer regions.

✅ Formatting Checklist

  • ✅
    Single-column layout — No multi-column layouts, no side-by-side sections. Pure single-column from top to bottom.
  • ✅
    No tables — Not even for organizing skills or contact info. Tables cause ATS parsing errors.
  • ✅
    No text boxes — Content in text boxes is often ignored by ATS parsers entirely.
  • ✅
    No graphics, icons, or images — No company logos, social media icons, skill rating bars, or photos (in US/UK/IN markets).
  • ✅
    Standard, ATS-safe font — Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Georgia, or Helvetica. 10–12pt body, 14–16pt name.
  • ✅
    Margins between 0.5 and 1 inch on all sides.
  • ✅
    Standard bullet points — Use only • (round bullet) or – (dash). Avoid uncommon bullet symbols that may not render in all ATS.
  • ✅
    Consistent spacing — Same spacing before and after each section, same indentation for all bullet points.
  • ✅
    No colored backgrounds or shaded sections — Keep the background white. ATS may struggle with colored areas.

✅ Sections Checklist

  • ✅
    Professional Summary present — 2–4 sentences at the top summarizing your value proposition with role-specific keywords.
  • ✅
    Work Experience section using exactly this header or "Experience" — reverse chronological order, most recent job first.
  • ✅
    Education section with degree name, institution, and year. Remove GPA if below 3.0 / 6.5 CGPA.
  • ✅
    Skills section clearly labeled — organized list of hard skills, technologies, tools, and relevant soft skills.
  • ✅
    Certifications section (if applicable) — full certification names with issuing body and date.
  • ✅
    All section headers are standard and recognizable — "Work Experience" not "My Professional Journey"; "Skills" not "Core Competencies".
  • ✅
    No blank or empty sections — If a section has nothing substantive, remove it entirely.

✅ Keywords Checklist

  • ✅
    Job title from JD matches or closely mirrors your target title in your summary and/or most recent job title.
  • ✅
    At least 60% of required keywords from the JD appear in your resume — Use ATSPro's JD Matcher to verify.
  • ✅
    Technical skills listed by exact name — "Python" not "programming language"; "AWS" not "cloud services".
  • ✅
    Industry-specific jargon is included — Use the same terminology the industry uses, not generic descriptions.
  • ✅
    Keywords appear naturally in context — Not just listed in skills, but used in bullet point sentences as well.
  • ✅
    Both full form and abbreviation included where applicable — "Artificial Intelligence (AI)"; "Project Management Professional (PMP)".
  • ✅
    No keyword stuffing — Keywords used naturally; not repeated 10 times with no context.

✅ Experience Bullet Points Checklist

  • ✅
    Every bullet point starts with a strong action verb — Led, Built, Developed, Generated, Achieved, Analyzed, etc.
  • ✅
    At least 50% of bullets contain a specific number — percentages, dollar amounts, user counts, time savings, team sizes.
  • ✅
    Employment dates are clearly formatted — "January 2022 – March 2024" or "Jan 2022 – Mar 2024". Not just years alone for recent positions.
  • ✅
    No unexplained gaps — If there's a gap of 6+ months, consider adding a brief note or including freelance/volunteer work for that period.
  • ✅
    Bullets describe outcomes, not just activities — "Reduced customer churn by 18%" not "Worked on customer retention".
  • ✅
    Current role uses present tense; all previous roles use past tense.

✅ Length Checklist

  • ✅
    1 page for 0–3 years experience, 2 pages for 4–10 years, 2–3 pages for 10+ years or executive roles.
  • ✅
    No excessive white space — The resume should not look sparse or padded. Fill available space with quality content.
  • ✅
    No content cut off at the bottom — Ensure all bullet points fit within the page limits and nothing is accidentally truncated.

✅ Final Review Checklist

  • ✅
    Zero spelling errors — Use Grammarly or similar. Typos in technical terms are particularly damaging as they break keyword matching.
  • ✅
    Zero grammar errors — Consistent grammar throughout. Professional tone.
  • ✅
    Consistent verb tense — Current role present tense, previous roles past tense, consistently applied throughout.
  • ✅
    ATS score verified — Upload to ATSPro and achieve 70+ before submitting. Use JD Matcher if the specific job posting is available.
  • ✅
    Resume tailored for this specific application — Summary, skills order, and key bullets reflect the language of this JD.

Use This Checklist Before Every Application

This checklist represents the cumulative research of studying how real ATS platforms evaluate resumes. Not every ATS weighs every factor identically, but all major systems — Workday, Taleo, Greenhouse, Lever, iCIMS — evaluate the fundamentals covered here: format clarity, keyword presence, section structure, contact completeness, and content quality.

The job seekers who consistently get callbacks in 2026 aren't necessarily the most qualified candidates — they're the candidates who have learned to communicate their qualifications in the precise language that both ATS algorithms and human recruiters respond to. That's a learnable skill. This checklist is your map.
