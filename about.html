Why We Built ATSPro

In 2024, one of our co-founders applied to 80+ companies over four months without a single callback. The resume was well-written, the experience was solid, and the applications went out on time. What nobody told him was that most of those applications never reached a human eye — they were silently filtered out by Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).

After that painful experience, we dug deep into how ATS software actually works. We talked to recruiters, HR managers, and hiring platform engineers. We reverse-engineered what makes a resume ATS-friendly. We built internal tools to test different resume formats. And slowly, an idea emerged — what if we made this knowledge accessible to everyone, for free?

ATSPro was born in early 2025 with a single mission: give every job seeker the unfair advantage of knowing exactly what ATS sees when it scans their resume — before they hit submit.

Today, ATSPro has helped over 50,000 job seekers improve their resumes, increase their ATS scores, and land more interviews. We're proud that it's completely free, works entirely in your browser, and never stores your personal data.

🎯 Our Mission

The modern hiring process is broken. Over 98% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS software, and studies show that 75% of qualified candidates are rejected by ATS before a human ever reads their resume. This isn't a skills problem — it's an information gap.

Our mission is simple: democratize access to ATS knowledge. We believe that a great candidate should never miss an opportunity just because they didn't know how to format a resume for a robot. Every job seeker — from a fresh graduate in India to a senior executive in the US — deserves to understand how the hiring system works and how to work within it.

We do this by building free, powerful tools that run entirely in your browser. No account required, no data collected, no paywalls. Just clear, actionable insights that help you put your best resume forward.

🛠 How ATSPro Works

ATSPro uses a sophisticated client-side analysis engine built in JavaScript. When you upload your resume, our parser extracts the full text using PDF.js (for PDFs) or Mammoth.js (for DOCX files) — entirely in your browser without any server communication.

The extracted text is then analyzed across 9 key categories: contact information completeness, section structure, action verb usage, quantified achievements, keyword density, resume length, formatting compatibility, file format suitability, and employment date formatting. Each category is scored and weighted to produce your overall ATS score out of 100.

Our Job Description Matcher feature goes one step further — it extracts keywords from a job posting you paste in, and compares them word-by-word against your resume to show exactly which keywords you're missing and need to add for that specific role.

All of this runs at browser speed with zero data exposure. It's the most private ATS checker available anywhere.
